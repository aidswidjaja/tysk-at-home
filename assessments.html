Assessment schedule

Accurate as of 20 August 2021

What you need

  • Microsoft Teams
  • Canvas
  • Microsoft Lens (requires setup beforehand)
  • Writing booklets from your care packs
  • Black pens, writing resources, notes, etc.

Assessment policies

  • Assessments will be online & open-book — open-book means you can use any resource, including print (e.g textbook), physical (e.g exercise book) and online (e.g Desmos)
  • They are timed and will run for 45 or 60 minutes, except for the Studies of Religion assessments, which are speaking assessments
  • You will be supervised through Microsoft Teams — cameras and microphones must be on at all times but you can turn your volume down
  • School uniform must be worn during the assessment
  • Stay at your assessment station for the entire assessment - don't go anywhere
  • No timetabled classes from 3 September to 14 September
  • Disability Provisions take place in their own Microsoft Teams room
  • You need to have signed the HSC Honesty in Assessment policy

In the assessment

  • 5 minutes before starting time: download assessment task from Canvas Notifications page
  • Questions will be on Canvas, but write answers in your writing booklets
  • Write the Assessment Code at the top of each writing page
  • 10 minutes after finishing time: upload answers to Canvas through Microsoft Lens

Studies of Religion

  • The Studies of Religion 1-unit and 2-unit assessments are Viva Voce speaking assessments
  • Basically, you have to have a discussion (usually with some questions asked by the examiner) for 4-5 or 7-8 minutes (depending on your course) about the assigned topic
  • Unlike other assessment tasks, SOR assessments are individually scheduled - check the notification for your speaking times
  • Instead of Microsoft Teams, use the allocated Zoom link for your speaking room (available on the notification)

Having problems?

  • When receiving the assessment if you are having problems, use the chat function
  • During the assessment if you are having problems, use the Raise Hand function on Microsoft Teams
